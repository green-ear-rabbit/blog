Clinical notes: A schizophrenia patient

2020-05-25

One good-weather afternoon when I sit in the public office and read a medical book sleepily.

There is a new patient just accepted by our department. It’s a patient with schizophrenia, with liver cirrhosis, and were sent to hospital by apsychia.

Considering schizophrenia, he was put in a separate and isolated ward.

I was just a student, and what I can do is only to go to ask patient’s history.

… … … … …

I feel very sorry to hear this patient history, even if I am not supposed to be so.

He is not local people in this city, neither for tourism. He was from a relief station. He wanted to find a job here, but cannot find a long term job even if he can do lots of labors. He is literate and speaking is clear.

He did not have any abnormal behaviors even if he has schizophrenia. After several days, we found we may don’t need to allocate him to a separate room and he’s totally a normal patients.

He said, schizophrenia, can be aggressive or just depressive, he is the later one. As he described, he never had any aggressive behavior.

However, why did the psychiatrist diagnose him as schizophrenia eight years ago, rather than just depression? Will the society, the factory accept a patient with schizophrenia?

I think the psychiatrist should be careful to give any diagnosis. I always against the over-diagnosis in mental health. Does ADHD a real disease? Does depression a real disease? Or even, does schizophrenia a real disease? I don’t think so. For ADHD, it’s just not same as other children. For depression, bipolar and schizophrenia, it’s just a status, rather than disease.

Also, once a person were diagnosed as a mental disorder, it becomes a label. As this patient, even if he don’t have any aggressive behavior, he may have difficult to find a job and have a better life.

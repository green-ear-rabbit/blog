A 19-year-old, rural, skinny girl diagnosed with lupus erythematosus.

Sometimes I met and talk with her in the ward. Some of our talking are very impressive to me, both reflect environmental and social issues.

After I take notes on her symptoms, I still want to ask more things, even if I am not supposed to do. I am always interested in how does people get disease. The lupus erythematosus, why does this disease come to her.

“Do you parents, grandparents, or any relatives has this disease?”

“Nope”

“Did you take any medicine before several weeks before the symtoms onset.”

“Nope”

“Do you have any symtoms before this month? Do you have other diseases when you are young?”

“No, never, I am healthy until I left my hometown and come to this city. And I only cold and fever when I am young, never other diseases”

“Left hometown for what?” I asked

“To work in a manicure store, and later I got symptoms on my nose three months later.”

I became interested in, and I asked, “So…how was the environment of that store? Is there any unusual smell?”

“Ah, Yes! There is the smell in that store, but you know, every manicure store have that smell. So the smell of manicure will cause my disease, right?”

“No… no, I don’t know”

Later I asked doctors in the office, will the smell of manicure cause lupus erythematosus?

I forgot what answer I got, but it was not a statisfied answer…
